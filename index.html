ðŸ’† The History & Magic of Massage

The benefits and magic of massage have been recognized across cultures and throughout history. The Egyptians were among the first to document massage techniques. Traditional Chinese Medicine has long recognized the importance of Qi flowing freely. Ayurvedic medicine places great emphasis on massage as part of daily rituals. Hippocrates, the Father of Medicine, recommended massage as part of his medical teachings.

The science of touch has evolved through research showing how these practices benefit both physical and psychological health. Touch stimulates the parasympathetic nervous system, triggers the release of endorphins and oxytocin, reduces anxiety, and supports overall well-being.

"Massage is so much more than just a luxury or indulgence - it's a therapeutic practice with profound benefits. The best time to get a massage is before you need one."

ðŸ’” Trauma & The Body

Trauma is real. It affects far more than your mind. It leaves fingerprints on your nervous system, weaves itself into the tissues of your body, and echoes in your thoughts, hormones, gut, breath, dreams, and relationships.

Your body remembers what the mind tries to forget. It stores itself in muscle memory, in tight jaws and clenched hips, in digestive issues and tension headaches, in the way you hold your breath when someone raises their voice. Complex trauma (CPTSD) is characterized by the persistence of reliving trauma in the nervous system.

Healing trauma is a sacred, layered, nonlinear process. It requires a holistic approach - one that honors body, mind, emotions, and spirit together. A one-size-fits-all method won't work here. This is deep work that unfolds on a timeline that can't be rushed.

"These aren't personality flaws. They're deeply human responses to prolonged or repeated wounding. Unprocessed trauma doesn't just fade - it imprints."
