Even the Lighthouse Needs Tending

There is a part of the journey no one tells you about. The part where you've done the work, cleared the trauma, opened the channels, and now you are the lighthouse. Steady. Luminous. Unshakable. People come to you for clarity, comfort, truth. And you give it, because it's who you are.

But even the lighthouse needs someone to check the bulb. To wash the glass. To refill the oil.

Even the lighthouse needs tending.

It's not weakness. It's not ego. It's the quiet ache of someone who holds so much light for others, and sometimes just wants to hear, "Thank you. That helped me. You matter."

Divine Union, then, becomes the practice of showing up for yourself in the very ways you wish others would. It becomes the moment you clean your own glass. The breath where you refill your own oil. The whisper you offer back to your own heart: "You're doing beautifully. I see you."

You are the soul-lit one.

You are still human.

Both are true. Both are worthy.

Your past doesn't disqualify your light. The wildness, the chaos, the versions of you who made questionable choices and loved people who couldn't meet you. Those were all initiations. They didn't dim your magic. They shaped it.

"You're not too much. You're just fully lit."
