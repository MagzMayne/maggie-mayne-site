Get your feet in the dirt

"Nature doesn't rush. The trees don't apologize for taking up space. The sun never questions whether it's worthy of shining. That kind of energy is powerful to be around."

Go get your feet in the dirt, the grass, the rivers, the lakes, and the ocean as often as possible. Connecting with nature offers us so many restorative benefits - mentally, emotionally, spiritually, and physically.

Spending time outdoors, whether it's a walk in the park, a hike in the woods, or simply sitting under a tree, can have incredibly calming and rejuvenating effects. Nature provides a sense of peace and presence that brings you back to yourself. It slows you down. It reminds you to breathe.

My Story

One of my favorite ways to move my body is by combining it with a beautiful view. I've done some of my deepest processing and most powerful manifesting on long walks through forests, alongside mountain streams, or while sitting at the edge of a lake with my journal. Even short walks in the fresh air - barefoot if possible - have shifted entire days for me.

When I was going through one of the most difficult chapters of my life, I made it a point to step outside every single morning - even if it was cold. I'd breathe in the air, say a few words of gratitude, and just let the wind remind me that I was still here.

"Still alive. Still moving forward."

The Benefits

Being outside is one of the best ways to regulate your nervous system. It can improve your physical activity, lower your blood pressure, and stabilize your heart rate.

Reduces risk of chronic conditions like diabetes and cardiovascular disease

Sunlight helps synthesize vitamin D for bone health and immune support

Stimulates the retinas and supports dopamine production

Reduces anxiety and regulates circadian rhythms

Boosts creativity and improves attention span

Even calms anger

Even in medical settings, patients who spend time in nature - or even just have a view of it - tend to heal faster, require fewer medications, and experience fewer complications.
